Sector Deep Dive: A Tale of Three Markets

The hiring climate varies dramatically across key sectors. While consulting faces significant headwinds due to economic uncertainty and project delays, corporate and banking roles show more resilience, driven by internal digital transformation and strategic growth initiatives.

The Money Question: Compensation in Flux

Total compensation is being reshaped. Firms are de-risking by holding base salaries steady while reducing variable pay like performance bonuses and signing bonuses, reflecting a more cautious market outlook.

The Skills Shift: What's In, What's Out

The demand for generalist strategists is waning, replaced by a surge in demand for specialists. Expertise in AI, data analytics, and sustainability are now table stakes for premium roles.

📈Trending Up

  • 🤖
    AI & Machine Learning Strategy: Integrating AI into business operations.
  • 📊
    Data Analytics & Visualization: Translating complex data into actionable insights.
  • 🌱
    ESG & Sustainability Consulting: Focusing on environmental impact and governance.
  • 💻
    Tech Implementation & Cloud Services: Hands-on digital transformation roles.

📉Trending Down

  • 🤔
    Generalist Corporate Strategy: Broad-stroke strategic planning without a tech focus.
  • 🏢
    Due Diligence (Volume): M&A slowdown has reduced the volume of diligence projects.
  • 📈
    Traditional Market Research: Being replaced by data-driven analytics approaches.
  • 👥
    Human Capital Generalists: Shift towards specialized HR tech and analytics roles.

Global Headwinds & Tailwinds

Hiring is not monolithic across the globe. Mature markets like the US and Europe are experiencing a cooldown, while growth markets in APAC and India, though cautious, still present pockets of opportunity.

🇺🇸 US & Canada

Status: Cooling. Major consulting firms have delayed start dates and reduced campus hiring. Tech sector layoffs have increased the MBA talent supply, making competition fierce.

🇪🇺 Europe

Status: Cautious. Similar trends to the US, with boutique and specialized firms faring better than large generalist consultancies. Financial services hiring in London remains subdued.

🇮🇳 India

Status: Mixed. Tech consulting and corporate strategy roles are still hiring, but with increased scrutiny on profitability. Start-up ecosystem slowdown has impacted analyst roles.

🌏 APAC

Status: Pockets of Growth. Singapore and other financial hubs show resilience. Wealth management is a key growth area, driven by increasing regional wealth.

The Road Ahead: An Actionable Playbook

Navigating this market requires a proactive and targeted approach for both candidates and hiring managers.

For Candidates

1
Specialize & Skill Up: Go deep on a high-demand skill like AI or ESG. Certifications in Python, Tableau, or cloud platforms are differentiators.
2
Network Beyond Tier 1: Explore boutique firms, corporate strategy roles, and private equity portfolio companies which have more immediate hiring needs.
3
Show, Don't Tell: Build a portfolio of projects. Use case studies from coursework or internships to demonstrate tangible impact and analytical skills.

For Hiring Managers

1
Rethink Role Profiles: Prioritize specific technical skills and adaptability over generalist prestige. Hire for demonstrable skills, not just school names.
2
Look for Talent in New Places: Expand recruiting beyond traditional target schools to find candidates with diverse backgrounds and specialized experience.
3
Focus on Retention: In a tight market, investing in upskilling current employees can be more effective than competing for a limited pool of new, expensive talent.
